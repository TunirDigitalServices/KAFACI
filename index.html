Smart Pest Management for Tomato Crops in Tunisia

Decision Support Systems for sustainable and chemical-reduced agriculture

Tomato Crop Threats

Tomatoes are a vital crop in Tunisia, cultivated year-round in open fields and greenhouses. They are severely threatened by pests and diseases, particularly Tuta absoluta.

Smart Pest Management

The KAFACI-PEST-FAW program, in partnership with CRRHAB, develops Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies and Decision Support Systems (DSS) to protect tomato crops while reducing chemical pesticide use.

How does it work?

  • Field & Greenhouse Monitoring

    Continuous monitoring of tomato crops to detect pest population dynamics.

  • Decision Support System (DSS)

    Data-driven tools support farmers in choosing optimal pest control strategies.

  • Integrated Pest Management

    Combining biological, ecological, and chemical methods to ensure sustainability.

Frequently Asked Questions

IPM is an ecosystem-based strategy focusing on long-term prevention of pests through biological control, habitat manipulation, and reduced pesticide use.

The DSS provides real-time data, alerts, and recommendations to help farmers make informed decisions on pest control, irrigation, and crop management while reducing chemical inputs.

Reducing pesticide use protects the environment, preserves beneficial insects, improves food safety, and supports sustainable agriculture, especially in water-scarce regions like Tunisia.
